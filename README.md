# babylon-mood-ring

The Babylon Mood Ring website styles and randomizes lines from "Frozen Custard in the City of Babylon" — a poem I wrote about one decade ago and had no use for, really, until now.
The site scrambles the poem's linear sequencing in favor of a more dynamic and stochastic reading experience. It does this by revealing lines at different screen coordinates and shifting colors, extending the poem's thematic focus on memory, nostalgia, and selfhood to the level of staging and design. In short, sampling random verses and styling text in accordance with changing moods formally mimics the impermanence and randomness of memories long past and over.

Inspiration for the project draws on the one hand from Kenneth Goldsmith's notion of "uncreative writing," which challenges traditional notions of originality and points to acts of selection, appropriation, combination, and context as more timely methods of (re)creation in our overdeveloped digital age. The website's mechanism of selecting lines at random puts this approach to work, displaying verses without a fixed order or structure to opens the possibility for new meanings and interpretations for both readers and myself. 

The approach also aligns with proposals for "stochastic poetics" that center randomness and probability in the creative production and audience reception of (e-)literature. The unpredictable, algorithmically randomized presentation of lines builds on this framework to engage with the amorphousness of memory and the various ways our past can manifest in the present as if at random.
