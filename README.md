# babylon-mood-ring

[Babylon Mood Ring](https://zmuhls.github.io/babylon-mood-ring/) styles and randomizes shards of language from "Frozen Custard in the City of Babylon" — a poem I wrote about one decade ago and had no use for, really, until now. 

I've created the site to scramble the poem's linear presentation in favor of a more dynamic and stochastic reading experience. To do this, the site reveals lines at different screen coordinates and shifting colors, mapping the poem's nonlinear and fractured approach to memory and self to the level of staging and design. Sampling random verses and styling text in accordance with changing moods formally mimics the impermanence and randomness of memories long past and over.

Inspiration for the project draws from Kenneth Goldsmith's concept of "uncreative writing," challenges traditional notions of originality and points to acts of selection, appropriation, combination, and context as more timely methods of (re)creation in our overdeveloped digital age. The website's mechanism of selecting lines at random puts this approach to work, displaying verses without a fixed order or structure to opens the possibility for new meanings and interpretations for both readers and myself. 

The approach also adopts Drucker's work in "stochastic poetics" to build randomness into the creative production — and reception — of literary works. The unpredictable, algorithmically randomized display of lines builds on this framework to reflect on the amorphousness of memory and the various ways our past can manifest in the present, as if at random.
